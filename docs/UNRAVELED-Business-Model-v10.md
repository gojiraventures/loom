## 1. Revenue Philosophy

**No advertising. Ever.**

Ads destroy credibility for this type of content instantly. The moment someone sees a banner ad on a page about Nephilim, they assume the content exists to generate ad clicks. Every conspiracy site on the internet runs ads. We don't.

**No corporate sponsors visible on content pages.**

If we partner with anyone, their support is acknowledged on a separate page — never inline with research content. The content must feel independent because it IS independent.

**Revenue comes from value, not attention.**

We make money because people find what we build genuinely valuable — not because we're harvesting their eyeballs for advertisers.

## 3. Content Protection

### What We're Protecting

Our value isn't individual facts (those are public domain). Our value is:
1. The structured cross-referencing and unraveled scoring
2. The curated primary source library with verified links
3. The Advocate/Skeptic analyses
4. The People Dossiers and relationship networks
5. The jaw-drop layers and editorial presentation
6. The original research connections nobody else has made

### Protection Strategies

**Legal:**
- All original content published under **Creative Commons BY-NC-SA 4.0** — free to share and adapt for non-commercial purposes with attribution and same license. This means: anyone can quote us, link to us, reference us (which helps GEO). Nobody can scrape our content and monetize it.
- **Terms of Service** explicitly prohibiting automated scraping, bulk downloading, and republication without attribution.
- **DMCA takedown** process for anyone who scrapes and republishes without attribution.

**Technical:**
- **Rate limiting** on all public pages — normal browsing is unaffected, but automated scraping gets throttled.
- **robots.txt** — Allow legitimate search engines and AI crawlers (this helps GEO). Block known scraper bots.
- **Fingerprinting** — Embed invisible unique identifiers in our content (specific word choices, invisible characters, unique formatting patterns). If someone scrapes and republishes, we can prove it's ours.
- **API as the legitimate access path** — If someone wants our structured data programmatically, we offer an API. Scraping the HTML is the illegitimate path.

**Cultural:**
- **Be so good they cite you.** If our content is genuinely the best-structured, best-sourced, best-cross-referenced resource on these topics, people will link to us rather than copy us. The AI engines will cite us rather than scrape us. Being the authoritative source is the best protection.
- **Update constantly.** Scraped content is a snapshot. Our content is live, updated, with verified links. The copy degrades over time. The original keeps getting better.

### What We WANT Scraped (Strategically)

We actually WANT AI models to index our content — that's how we get cited in AI-generated responses. The key is controlling what they index:

- **Allow:** Googlebot, Bingbot, GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended (Gemini)
- **Block:** Generic scrapers, content farms, mirror sites
- **Serve structured data:** JSON-LD schema markup on every page so AI engines can parse our content cleanly

## 4. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) Strategy

### Why Unraveled Is Perfectly Positioned for GEO

The Princeton research paper (2024) that coined "GEO" found that content with **specific statistics, named citations, and structured data** gets cited by AI engines 30-40% more than unoptimized content. Our entire site is built this way:

- Every claim has a named source with credentials
- Every statistic is specific ("268 flood narratives across 6 continents")
- Every topic has structured shared elements, tradition comparisons, and evidence indices
- We have original data that doesn't exist anywhere else (convergence scores, cross-cultural structural element matrices, People Dossiers)

**We don't need to optimize for AI citation — we need to not screw it up.** The content is already structured for it.

### GEO Tactics (Specific to Unraveled)

**1. BLUF structure (Bottom Line Up Front)**
Every page should open with a direct, citable answer. When someone asks ChatGPT "how many flood narratives exist worldwide," the AI's sub-query hits our Flood page. Our opening paragraph must directly answer that question in the first 2-3 sentences with a specific number and source.

**2. FAQ sections on every topic page**
AI engines heavily weight clear question-and-answer pairs. Each topic should have a "Frequently Asked Questions" section with questions people actually ask:
- "How many flood stories exist worldwide?"
- "What is a biblically accurate angel?"
- "What are the Nephilim?"
- "How many pyramids exist outside Egypt?"
- "What did the Smithsonian do with giant skeletons?"

Each answer: 2-3 sentences, specific, cited.

**3. Entity authority**
Build a strong entity presence for "Unraveled" as a brand. This means:
- Consistent naming ("Unraveled — Cross-Tradition Evidence Index")
- Author pages with credentials
- Schema markup (Organization, WebSite, Article, FAQPage, Person)
- Wikipedia page (when notable enough)
- Consistent presence across platforms (Substack, YouTube, social)

**4. Original data that only we have**
AI engines cite what they can't find elsewhere. Our unique data:
- Unraveled scores (nobody else computes these)
- Cross-cultural structural element matrices
- People Dossiers with credibility ratings
- The Irrefutable Facts Database
- Scripture side-by-side comparisons in original languages

**5. Freshness signals**
Every page shows "Last Updated: [date]" prominently. Refresh content quarterly with new findings, updated links, and new unraveled connections.

**6. Third-party citations (earned media)**
AI engines weight third-party mentions heavily. Strategies:
- Get cited by academic researchers (our primary sources and methodology are citable)
- Get featured on podcasts (Joe Rogan, Lex Fridman, etc.)
- Get mentioned in Reddit communities (r/AcademicBiblical, r/Archaeology, r/mythology)
- Get reviewed by scholars and researchers who can validate our methodology

## 5. Data Sources — Including Substack

### Substack as Intelligence Source

Substack is a goldmine for our research because many serious independent researchers publish there — people who are too heterodox for mainstream journals but too rigorous for YouTube conspiracy channels. Exactly our target zone.

**How we use Substack:**

| Method | What It Gets Us |
|--------|----------------|
| **RSS monitoring** | Subscribe to relevant Substacks, auto-crawl new posts for claim extraction |
| **Author tracking** | Add Substack authors to our People database with credibility scoring |
| **Cross-referencing** | When a Substack post cites a primary source, follow the citation chain back to the original |
| **Signal detection** | Substack writers often surface connections and patterns before mainstream outlets. They're early signal. |

**Substacks to monitor (starter list):**
- Independent archaeology and ancient history writers
- Religious studies scholars publishing outside traditional journals
- Comparative mythology and folklore researchers
- Science journalists covering archaeology and anthropology
- Alternative history researchers who actually cite sources

**The credibility filter:** Same Person Credibility system applies. A Substack writer with a PhD who cites primary sources gets Tier B-C. A Substack writer with no credentials who cites YouTube videos gets Tier D-E. The platform doesn't determine credibility — the sourcing does.

### Other Intelligence Sources to Ingest

| Source | How to Access | What It Gives Us |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Substack** | RSS feeds + web scraping of public posts | Independent researcher analysis, early signal |
| **Medium** | RSS + web scraping | Similar to Substack but broader, more noise |

## 6. Growth Strategy

### Phase 1 — Build Authority (Months 1-6)

**Goal:** Become the single best-sourced resource on 3 topics (Flood, Angels, Nephilim).

- Publish deep dives with jaw-drop layers
- Structure all content for GEO from day one
- Build the irrefutable facts database to 50+ entries
- Establish presence on relevant subreddits (provide value, don't spam)

**Metric targets:** 10K monthly visitors, first AI citation appearances.

**Goal:** Launch community features, establish Unraveled as a brand.

- Launch "The Signal" submissions system

**Metric targets:** 50K monthly visitors, 500 members, first $5K revenue month.

**Goal:** All 10 topics published. Intelligence engine operational. Revenue sustainable.

- All 10 pillar topics live with full deep dives

- 200+ irrefutable facts indexed
- API launched for researchers
- First annual report published
- Book proposal based on the research
- Documentary conversations started

**Metric targets:** 200K monthly visitors, 2000 members, $15-25K revenue month.

## 7. What We DON'T Do

| Never | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Run ads** | Destroys credibility instantly |
| **Accept corporate sponsorship on content pages** | Content must be visibly independent |
| **Paywall the research** | The mission is to make this information accessible. If we gate it, we become what we criticize. |
| **Sell user data** | Privacy-first analytics only |
| **Publish unverified claims for traffic** | Short-term clicks destroy long-term authority |
| **Take sides** | The Advocate and Skeptic both get published. We never editorialize a verdict. |
| **Chase algorithm trends** | We publish on our schedule based on research quality, not platform algorithm incentives |
| **Compromise sourcing for speed** | Better to publish one well-sourced piece per month than four poorly-sourced pieces per week |
